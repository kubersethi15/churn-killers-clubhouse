============================================================

Stop asking customers what they want.

Start asking what they're trying to accomplish.

When a customer says "I need better reporting," most CSMs write it down and move on.

Wrong move.

Dig deeper:

"What decision are you trying to make that current reports don't support?"

"What happens when you can't make that decision quickly?"

"How much time do you spend creating manual reports?"

"What would change if you had this data instantly?"

The surface complaint ("better reporting") is useless.

The underlying need ("I need to prove ROI to my boss monthly or my budget gets cut") is gold.

One tells you nothing about urgency, impact, or willingness to pay.

The other tells you everything.

This is the difference between collecting complaints and extracting insights.

What question do you wish your CSMs asked more often?
