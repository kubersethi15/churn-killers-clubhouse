## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Are your CSMs actually delivering customer insights or just complaints dressed up as intelligence?

I've been in Customer Success for years and just realized something uncomfortable: most of what we call "customer insights" are just complaints with better packaging.

Every quarter it's the same report: customers want better integrations, faster support, more training. We present this as voice of customer intelligence but it's really just surface-level noise.

Real insight would be: "Customers experiencing load times over 3 seconds during month two have 60% higher churn rates." That's predictive and actionable.

"The platform is slow" is just a complaint.

The difference is asking better questions. Instead of accepting "we need better reporting," dig deeper: "What decision are you trying to make? What happens when you can't make it quickly?"

I think most CS teams have become professional excuse-makers instead of intelligence operators. We're so focused on being customer advocates that we've stopped being business analysts.

Anyone else noticing this pattern? How do you train your team to extract real insights vs collect complaints?
