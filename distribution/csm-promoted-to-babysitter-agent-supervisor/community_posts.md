## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** An AI agent emailing customers at midnight didn't kill the CSM role. It exposed it.

There's a write-up going around where an AI system ran 150+ customer accounts and autonomously emailed 83 customers at 12:20am with personalised check-ins. The takeaway a lot of people landed on was that the CSM role is dead.

I think that's the wrong read, but for an uncomfortable reason.

Those accounts were transactional. One buyer, annual renewal, value the buyer could already count. That is the easiest possible work to automate, and honestly the agent probably does it better than a tired human writing check-ins between meetings.

What the agent can't do is the hard part. Put it on an enterprise account with a board-level budget fight, a champion who just left, a security flag freezing the expansion, and internal politics over who owns the tool. It can email everyone perfectly. Nothing moves.

So the role doesn't die. It splits. One half is supervising the agents and catching bad sends. The other half is closing the deals the machine can't touch. Most people in CS were hired for the relationship layer that sits right in the middle, which is exactly the part getting absorbed.

The scary question is how much of your current week is motion vs judgment. If your job is mostly cadence and CRM hygiene, the math isn't great.

I dug into the whole thing in my newsletter if anyone wants the longer version, but mostly curious how others here are thinking about it.
