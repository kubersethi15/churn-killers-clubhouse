# X Thread -- The Day Your CSM Got Promoted to Babysitter
# Post each tweet as a reply to the previous one

--- Tweet 1 ---
An AI agent emailed 83 customers at midnight with custom check-ins. No human in the loop. Everyone called it the death of the CSM role.

They were half right. The role doesn't die. It splits. And most teams are about to pick the wrong half.

--- Tweet 2 ---
Those 83 emails worked because the accounts were transactional. Single buyer. Annual renewal. Obvious value.

That's exactly the work software beats a tired human at. No argument.

--- Tweet 3 ---
Now put that agent on a Fortune 500 account. Board-level budget fight. Champion just left. Security froze the expansion. Three execs knifing each other over ownership.

The agent can email all of them at 12:20am. None of it moves the renewal an inch.

--- Tweet 4 ---
So the job splits into two.

Supervisor: babysits the agents, catches bad sends, manages by exception. Lower leverage.

Closer: owns the accounts agents can't touch. Sits in the room where renewals are decided. Higher leverage.

Most CSMs were hired for neither.

--- Tweet 5 ---
The number that decides your seat: your judgment ratio. The share of your week a machine can't replicate.

Run the audit before the org chart runs it for you.
