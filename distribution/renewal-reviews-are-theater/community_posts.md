## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Are we measuring the wrong things in customer success?

I've been thinking about how much time we spend in CS building renewal forecasts and health scores. All these engagement metrics, usage data, satisfaction scores.

But I'm starting to wonder if we're looking at trailing indicators and calling them predictive. Like, when email engagement drops, is that causing churn risk or revealing that churn risk already exists?

Worked with a company recently that had 23 different data points in their renewal tracking. Sophisticated stuff. But their prediction accuracy was 47%. Worse than flipping a coin.

Meanwhile, the real renewal decisions were happening in budget meetings they weren't invited to. Champion changes they found out about on LinkedIn. Procurement discussions where price was the only factor.

Maybe we're solving the wrong problem? Instead of better forecasting, maybe we need better influence earlier in the process.

I dive deeper into this pattern in my newsletter: https://churnisdead.com/newsletter/renewal-reviews-are-theater
