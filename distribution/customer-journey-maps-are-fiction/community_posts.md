## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Do your most successful customers actually follow your designed customer journey?

I've been analyzing customer behavior patterns at several B2B companies and noticed something interesting. The customers who achieve the highest ROI and expand the most rarely follow the journey we've mapped out for them. They skip stages we consider critical. They find shortcuts we didn't design. They hit milestones way earlier or later than expected.

Meanwhile, the customers who follow our documented journey perfectly often end up churning. They check all the boxes, hit every milestone on schedule, then quietly disappear.

This makes me think we're approaching journey mapping backwards. Instead of designing the ideal path and trying to guide customers along it, maybe we should study how our best customers naturally move through our product and adapt our process to support their behavior.

Has anyone else noticed this pattern? How do you balance having a structured onboarding process with the reality that every enterprise customer has unique needs and constraints? I dove deeper into this observation in my newsletter: https://churnisdead.com/newsletter/customer-journey-maps-are-fiction
