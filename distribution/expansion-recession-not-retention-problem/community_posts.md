## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Falling NRR is being blamed on CS when it's really an expansion problem. Anyone else seeing this?

Looking at the latest SaaS retention data, median NRR dropped from around 105% in 2021 to 101% in 2024. The detail that jumped out at me: gross retention barely moved during that slide.

That means customers are not churning. They just stopped buying more. Upsell, cross-sell, and price increases have all stalled, and AI is making buyers question whether they even need to renew at the same level.

Here is what bugs me. NRR is a single blended number, but in a real book it is doing three different jobs at once. You have flat-but-healthy accounts that adopted everything and have nothing left to buy. You have accounts that only grew because a contractual price escalator finally got enforced. And you have a thin slice of genuine net-new expansion. CS only really controls that last slice, but they own the whole number when it falls.

So when leadership sets a raw NRR target for next year, the CS team signs up to be blamed for a decline that is mostly a pricing-power and roadmap problem they cannot touch.

I think the fix is decomposing the number before planning so the conversation becomes about the ceiling, not the CSM. Curious whether anyone here has actually done that and how leadership reacted. I dug into the breakdown in my newsletter if useful, but mostly want to know if others are seeing the same thing.
