--- Tweet 1 ---
Median SaaS NRR fell from 105% to 101% since 2021.

Here is the part nobody says out loud: gross retention barely moved.

Customers are not leaving. They just stopped buying more.

That changes who is responsible.

--- Tweet 2 ---
Gross retention flat = customers staying.
Net retention falling = they stopped expanding.

Two completely different failures. The second one is not yours.

Upsell stalled. Cross-sell stalled. Price increases stalled. That is a pricing and roadmap problem, not a QBR problem.

--- Tweet 3 ---
Your blended NRR is one number doing three jobs:

1. Accounts that adopted everything and are done. Flat and healthy.
2. Accounts that grew only from a price escalator clause.
3. A thin slice of real net-new expansion.

You control one of those three.

--- Tweet 4 ---
So stop walking into planning with one number. Bring three.

The Floor (gross retention).
The Clause (price escalators).
The Lever (real expansion you drove).

In one room, the gap between expansion run and expansion possible was bigger than the entire net-expansion line.
