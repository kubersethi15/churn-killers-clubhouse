## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Is AI in Customer Success actually just disguised layoffs?

Seeing a troubling pattern across CS teams. Companies deploy AI claiming it will make CSMs "more strategic" but what actually happens is portfolio sizes double while headcount gets cut.

Just heard about a CSM who went from 15 accounts to 62 overnight because "AI handles routine check-ins." Within two weeks, three accounts escalated to cancel. Not product issues - implementation questions that got lost in AI routing.

When management was asked about hiring help: "We just invested $180K in AI, we need to see ROI first."

Salesforce cut 4,000 CS roles citing AI deployment. Workday eliminated 400 customer operations positions. The pattern is clear: deploy AI, reduce headcount, claim productivity gains.

The remaining CSMs aren't getting strategic work. They're drowning in expanded portfolios while customers get worse support.

AI can handle email sequences and health scores. It cannot handle relationship building, complex problem-solving, or reading between the lines when customers are frustrated.

The best CS teams use AI for insights and admin tasks, not to replace relationship managers.

Anyone else experiencing this? How are you pushing back against AI-justified headcount reductions?
