## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Are QBRs actually helping customer relationships or just creating busy work?

Been thinking about this after seeing some brutal data from an enterprise CS team. They sent 847 QBR invites last quarter. 312 customers declined immediately. 298 rescheduled. Of the meetings that happened, only 31 customers came prepared.

Most were just CSMs presenting usage data that customers already monitor themselves. Everyone nodding politely while checking other apps.

What's weird is that companies with the highest retention rates (Datadog, Snowflake, MongoDB) don't do QBRs at all. They built customer success into their product architecture. Insights arrive automatically. Expansion surfaces based on usage patterns.

Starting to think QBRs are relationship maintenance for relationships that shouldn't need maintenance. The strongest customer connections happen when success feels effortless.

Curious what others think. Are we creating ceremony around value that should be invisible?

I explored this more in a recent analysis: https://churnisdead.com/newsletter/fire-your-qbr-what-to-do-instead
