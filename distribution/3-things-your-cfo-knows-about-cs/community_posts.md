## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** CFO cut CS team 40% despite hitting every metric - what financial blind spots am I missing?

My team delivered 95% CSAT, 112% NRR, and 8.5 average health score. CFO still cut headcount 40%. I'm trying to understand what I'm missing from a financial perspective.

Turns out CFOs look at fully-loaded CS costs differently. Where I calculated $0.20 per retained dollar (salary/ARR), they calculated $0.55 including benefits, tools, overhead, opportunity cost.

Also learned that 88% of our NRR expansion came from 2 enterprise accounts that were expanding regardless. Strip that out and we influenced 2% growth while costing 8% of revenue.

Anyone else experienced this disconnect between CS performance metrics and financial reality? How do you bridge the gap between customer success and CFO expectations?

Starting to think we need to speak their financial language, not just report customer happiness scores.
