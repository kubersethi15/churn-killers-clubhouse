## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Is anyone else seeing AI implementations lead to massive CS team cuts?

I've been tracking this pattern across multiple companies and it's getting hard to ignore. The playbook seems to be:

1. Announce AI investment to "transform" customer success
2. Show demos of AI handling 70-80% of interactions
3. Cut CS headcount by similar percentages 2-4 weeks later
4. Call it "strategic transformation" and "focusing on high-value work"

Oracle cut 30k, Block admitted AI drove 4k layoffs, Salesforce eliminated 6k roles. CS teams seem to be getting hit hardest because the work is most automatable.

The uncomfortable truth? When AI can resolve 80% of customer inquiries without humans, the math becomes simple. You need 80% fewer people.

I'm not anti-AI, but I think we need to be honest about what's happening instead of pretending this is about "augmentation." The chatbot doesn't need supervision. The automated onboarding doesn't need human backup.

Has anyone seen this pattern at their company? What's the actual "strategic work" that's supposed to justify keeping current headcount?
