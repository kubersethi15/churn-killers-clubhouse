## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** CS platforms: Are we paying $200K for glorified spreadsheets?

Genuine question for CS folks: How much of your expensive CS platform do you actually use?

I keep seeing teams that pay $200K+ annually for comprehensive CS platforms but still need to check 2-3 different systems to answer basic customer questions. The platforms have amazing demos with AI predictions and automated workflows, but in reality most teams use maybe 30% of the features.

Meanwhile I can get ChatGPT to analyze customer health and draft renewal strategies in 30 seconds with no configuration.

Not trying to bash CS platforms entirely, but wondering if we're optimizing for vendor feature lists instead of what actually helps customers. Sometimes feels like we're paying premium prices for very sophisticated project management tools.

What's been your experience? Are these platforms delivering the ROI they promise, or are we missing something simpler?
