## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** How much of your NRR target can your CS team actually move?

I keep coming back to a stat from Benchmarkit's 2026 report. Usage-based SaaS companies retain at roughly 108%. Seat-based ones at roughly 95%. Same buyers, same economy. The only real difference is how the contract is shaped.

That's a thirteen-point gap that no CSM closes with a better QBR. It's set by pricing architecture before anyone opens their laptop.

So when leadership hands CS an NRR target and treats it as a measure of team performance, a big part of that number was decided in a pricing meeting CS was never in.

What makes this urgent now is agentic AI. When customers deploy AI agents, seat count drops while satisfaction stays high or even rises. On a seat-based contract, your best accounts can retain perfectly and still contract. You take the hit for a design decision you never made. We've already seen it in support (Klarna, Salesforce, Intercom rebuilding pricing) and it's moving into knowledge-worker tools.

My takeaway is to stop treating NRR as one number. Split it into the part you inherited from pricing and the part you can actually influence, then figure out who owns the rest.

Curious how others think about this. Do you own your full NRR number, or have you found a way to separate what's yours? I dug into it more in my newsletter but genuinely want to hear how people are handling it in the wild.
