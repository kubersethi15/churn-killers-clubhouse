Most CS teams treat the dashboard as if it covers the whole renewal.

It doesn't. It covers product reality. People logging in. Features adopted. Support load. All real. All measurable.

But here's what your stack has zero sensors pointed at:

The budget meeting where a new CFO rationalizes the vendor stack.

The org reshuffle that retires your champion's role.

The private Slack where a peer recommends a competitor.

The board mandate that reallocates spend toward an AI initiative.

The strategic decision to consolidate platforms.

None of that crosses the boundary your instrumentation can reach. It was never built to.

So when a green account churns, the question everyone asks is "why didn't the model catch it." Wrong question. The model reported faithfully on the only thing it could see.

The better question: how much of what walked out your door was ever yours to catch in the first place?

That number tells you whether you're running customer success or just watching a very confident graph.

Off-telemetry churn is the lost ARR your stack never flagged. Here's where it hides:

1. The budget cycle
A CFO rationalizing spend doesn't log in to tell you. The decision happens in a spreadsheet you'll never see.

2. The org chart
Your champion gets promoted, reorged, or replaced. The relationship that carried the account evaporates. Usage stays flat right up to the cancel.

3. The board mandate
A new strategic priority gets funded by clearing vendor spend. Your product becomes a line item to cut, not a tool to evaluate.

4. The peer conversation
Your buyer asks three people in their network what they use. You're not in that thread. You never will be.

5. The consolidation play
"We're standardizing on one platform." It's not about your product. That's exactly the problem.

None of these show up as a usage dip. None trigger a support spike. The health score stays green because the model is measuring the wrong territory.

The fix isn't a better model. It's admitting the dashboard is a map, not the territory, and building human coverage for the half no sensor reaches.

Because the tool didn't fail. We did. We let a confident green health score do our thinking for us. We stopped asking the customer hard questions because the graph looked calm. We confused instrumentation with intimacy.

The lesson stuck. A green dashboard is not a renewed account. It's a partial picture, confidently rendered.
