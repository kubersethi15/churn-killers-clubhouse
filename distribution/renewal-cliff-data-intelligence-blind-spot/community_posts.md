# Community Cross-Posts -- The Renewal Cliff Nobody Charted: Why CS Doesn't Own Half the Churn It's Blamed For

## Slack (Gain Grow Retain / CS Insider)

Had a realization recently that's been bugging me. We always blame the health score model when a green account churns. But on the biggest loss I ever worked, the model was completely right. It measured product reality faithfully. The account just got killed in a budget meeting we were never in, by a new CFO consolidating vendors. No usage signal could have caught that. It made me think half of churn doesn't even live in our data. It lives in the customer's org chart and budget cycle, where we have zero sensors. Curious if others have lived this. How do you cover the churn your dashboard structurally can't see? I wrote more about this here: https://churnisdead.com/newsletter/renewal-cliff-data-intelligence-blind-spot

## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Half the churn CS gets blamed for was never visible in any dashboard. Change my mind.

Been thinking about this after losing a seven-figure account that was green on every metric for over a year. Usage up, tickets down, power user who loved us.

Then ninety days notice. Turns out a new CFO had decided to consolidate the vendor stack eleven weeks earlier, in a meeting we were never in, to free up budget for an AI project the board wanted funded.

Nothing about that touched our telemetry. There was no usage dip to catch, no support spike, no NPS drop. The signal that killed the renewal lived entirely inside their org chart and budget cycle.

What bugs me is that everyone kept asking how the model missed it. The model didn't miss it. It reported faithfully on the only thing it could see, which is product engagement. We missed it because we treated the dashboard as the full picture when it only covers maybe a quarter of what actually decides a renewal.

Vendors keep selling 85 percent churn prediction accuracy. But that accuracy only applies to the churn that lives in your data. It says nothing about the churn that gets decided in rooms you'll never be in.

Curious how other CS folks handle this. Do you build explicit coverage for budget cycles and champion succession, or are you also just trusting the graph? I ended up writing a full breakdown of this in my newsletter but mostly I want to know if I'm wrong here.
