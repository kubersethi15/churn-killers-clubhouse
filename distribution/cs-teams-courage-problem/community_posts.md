## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Why can CS teams predict churn perfectly but never prevent it?

Been thinking about this paradox in our industry. Customer Success teams have built these incredibly sophisticated health scores and predictive models. We can tell you with 87% accuracy which customers will churn in the next 90 days.

But when those predictions come true, we act surprised. The elaborate early warning systems failed to trigger any meaningful intervention.

I think the problem is we optimized for customer comfort instead of customer outcomes. We became really good at making customers feel heard about their problems, but terrible at actually solving them.

Enterprise customers paying $200K annually aren't looking for vendors who validate their feelings. They want advisors who drive results. But CS teams have convinced themselves that being customer-centric means never making customers uncomfortable.

Meanwhile, their usage stays low, their executive sponsor gets fired, their budget gets cut, and they blame the economy.

Anyone else seeing this pattern? How do you balance being customer-centric with being outcome-focused?
