## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** How do you structure your CS executive reviews to actually drive decisions?

I've been questioning the traditional QBR format lately. Most teams spend weeks building slide decks about trailing metrics and historical performance. Executives sit through presentations about problems they can't fix and trends they can't influence.

Meanwhile, the decisions that will determine next quarter's outcomes happen in real-time without executive input.

I'm experimenting with Monthly Business Reviews focused on forward-looking resource allocation instead of backward-looking performance analysis. Four key questions: What obstacles prevent customer success this month? Which resource gaps will create problems? What initiatives need executive support? Which customer decisions need our intervention?

The shift is from explaining what happened to deciding what happens next. Early results are promising but curious what others have tried. How do you structure your CS executive meetings to actually drive business decisions rather than just reporting on outcomes?

I wrote up the full framework transition process if anyone's interested: https://churnisdead.com/newsletter/fire-your-qbr-heres-what-to-do-instead
