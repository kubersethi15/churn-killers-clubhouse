# X Thread -- Fire Your QBR. Here's What to Do Instead.
# Post each tweet as a reply to the previous one

--- Tweet 1 ---
A CS leader spent 40 hours preparing QBRs last quarter. Detailed slide decks. Health scores trending green. Win/loss analysis. Two weeks after the final QBR, their biggest customer churned. The competitive evaluation started 6 weeks earlier. It never came up in any review.

--- Tweet 2 ---
QBRs assume looking backward helps you move forward. They assume quarterly reporting aligns with customer decision timelines. They assume executive attention is best spent on historical analysis. All wrong. Customers make decisions when conditions change, not when you report.

--- Tweet 3 ---
While you're building slide decks, customers are building vendor evaluations. While you're analyzing last quarter's NRR, they're scheduling competitor demos. While you're presenting improvements, they're preparing to leave. Your QBR schedule doesn't determine their timeline.

--- Tweet 4 ---
Traditional QBRs encourage performance theater. Teams spend weeks explaining why metrics moved instead of identifying what obstacles executives can remove. The highest-leverage meeting becomes the least impactful. Historical analysis instead of forward-looking decisions.

--- Tweet 5 ---
Replace QBRs with Monthly Business Reviews focused on decisions, not reporting. What obstacles prevent customer success this month? Which resource gaps need filling? What strategic initiatives need support? Full framework: https://churnisdead.com/newsletter/fire-your-qbr-heres-what-to-do-instead
