Your CS dashboard is lying to you.

I just talked to a CS leader whose "healthy" customers churned $2.3M in Q4. NPS was 67. Health scores were 85+. Engagement was trending up.

She was measuring the show, not the show results.

NPS measures sentiment in isolation. Renewal decisions happen in context: budget cuts, new priorities, stakeholders who don't care about your support scores.

Health scores? Just weighted averages of more vanity metrics. You're measuring product health when you need business health.

The uncomfortable truth: you're confusing activity with outcomes. Theater with performance.

Time to stop measuring motion and start measuring what matters.

Stop tracking engagement. Start tracking outcomes.

Here's what actually predicts renewal:

• Time to first value achieved (not first login)
• Progress toward customer's stated business goals
• ROI delivered vs ROI promised during sales
• Stakeholder expansion within the account
• Feature adoption tied to specific use cases
• Resolution of implementation blockers

Engagement without results is just expensive user research for your competitors.

A customer can log in 50 times a week and still churn if they're not achieving their goals.

Another customer might barely touch your product but renew at 150% because you solved their core problem.

The difference? One measures motion. The other measures progress.

Which category do your current metrics fall into?
