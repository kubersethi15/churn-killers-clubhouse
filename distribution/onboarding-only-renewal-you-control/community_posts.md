# Community Cross-Posts -- Onboarding Isn't the Start of Retention. It's the Only Renewal You'll Ever Fully Control.

## Slack (Gain Grow Retain / CS Insider)

Been chewing on something this week. We treat onboarding as an operational cost to minimise, and I think that is quietly killing renewals nobody blames on onboarding.

The logic: every retention lever after go-live is shared. Pricing is the CRO's. Expansion is the AE's. Roadmap is Product's. Onboarding is the one phase where CS actually has unilateral control over the outcome. And we optimise it for speed to hit go-live targets.

The reframe that stuck with me is depth of root. Did the customer build a dependency they cannot easily rip out, or did we just deliver a login? A login churns quietly eleven months later when a champion leaves. A root survives it.

Curious how others measure onboarding beyond time to value. I wrote more about this here: https://churnisdead.com/newsletter/onboarding-only-renewal-you-control

## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Anyone else think we measure onboarding completely wrong?

Been in CS for a while and something has been bugging me. We measure onboarding almost entirely by speed. Time to first value, time to go-live, time to activation. All of it optimises for the day the account moves from implementing to live in the CRM and stops eating our hours.

But none of those numbers tell you whether the customer actually built something they cannot walk away from.

The pattern I keep seeing: the accounts that hurt when they churn are not the dramatic ones. Product failures and pricing fights you see coming. The ones that sting are the quiet accounts that went live early, looked fine, and left in month eleven when a new VP showed up or the champion left. They were never rooted. We delivered a login and called it done.

What I am starting to believe is that onboarding is the only phase where CS has real unilateral control. Everything after go-live is a negotiation with Sales, Product, or the customer's internal politics. So spending that phase racing to a go-live target feels like borrowing against a renewal you will lose later.

How do you all measure onboarding beyond speed? Has anyone found a way to track depth or dependency? I ended up writing a longer take on this in my newsletter but genuinely want to hear how other teams handle it.
