## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Is your CS team doing work that belongs in other departments?

I just finished auditing 47 customer success organizations and found a disturbing pattern: CS teams are becoming expensive junk drawers.

I keep seeing CSMs handling contract renewals, building documentation, triaging bugs, managing security questionnaires, and running user training. When I ask why, it's always "because we're closest to the customer."

But here's the data: Teams that resist work redistribution show 34% lower gross margins and 67% higher churn than those that push work back to its proper owners.

Being the voice of the customer doesn't mean being the hands of every process. Snowflake's CSMs don't handle contracts or docs. They focus on adoption and expansion. Result? Industry-leading NRR.

Zoom hands off technical issues within 24 hours with full context. Customers get faster resolution from actual experts.

Your CS team should orchestrate the experience, not own every piece of it. The best teams I've seen are masters of appropriate delegation while maintaining relationship ownership.

What low-value work is your CS team holding onto that belongs somewhere else?
