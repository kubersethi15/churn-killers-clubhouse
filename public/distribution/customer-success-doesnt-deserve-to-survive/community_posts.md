## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** What actually makes a CS team "uncuttable"? Looking for honest perspectives.

After watching the Workday and Salesforce layoffs hit CS teams specifically, I've been thinking hard about what separates the teams that survive restructuring from the ones that don't.

The pattern I keep seeing in teams that are untouchable comes down to five things. Revenue attribution (not just being "involved" in renewals, but being able to point to specific dollars you influenced). Technical credibility (being able to sit in an architecture review and add value, not just schedule the meeting). Decision shaping (did the customer actually make a different decision because of your input?). Veto power (can you flag bad-fit deals before they close?). And proof of absence (would something operational break if you disappeared?).

The hardest one for me was the last one. I ran the "proof of absence" test on my own accounts. For 7 out of 10, the honest answer was: they'd be fine without me. Nothing operational would break.

That's the gap between being liked and being necessary.

I wrote a longer piece breaking this down with specific action steps if anyone's interested: https://churnisdead.com/newsletter/customer-success-doesnt-deserve-to-survive

But genuinely curious: what do you think makes a CS team impossible to cut? What's your experience been?
