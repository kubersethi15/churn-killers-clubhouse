## Reddit (r/CustomerSuccess)

**Title:** Mapped my CSM week into "AI could do this" vs "only I can do this." The results were humbling.

Did something uncomfortable last week. Went through every task in a typical CSM week and honestly labeled each one: could an AI with access to our CRM, product data, and support history do this?

The answer for about 60% of my week was yes. Data pulls for QBRs. Status update emails. Internal reports. Meeting prep. Health score reviews. All of it could be automated today with existing tools.

The 40% that survived was genuinely different work. Telling a customer their deployment approach was going to fail based on patterns I've seen across dozens of accounts. Reframing a vendor consolidation decision in terms the customer's CFO would actually care about. Pushing back internally on a deal that was going to become my churn number in 6 months.

The common thread in the surviving 40%: judgment, trust, and willingness to say uncomfortable things. That's hard to automate.

I think the real AI question for CS isn't "will it take my job" but "how much of my current job was ever worth protecting." For me the answer was about 40%. Working on making it higher.

Anyone else done this kind of audit on their own role? What did you find?
